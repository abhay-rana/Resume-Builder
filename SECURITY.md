# Security Policy

## Our Commitment

ResumeFreePro is committed to protecting your data and privacy. We take security seriously and have implemented measures to ensure your information remains safe.

---

## Data Handling

### What We Collect

| Data Type | Stored Where | Purpose |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Resume content | Your browser (localStorage) | Persist your work |
| Usage analytics | Anonymous, aggregated | Improve the product |

### What We Don't Collect

- Personal identification information on our servers
- Credit card or payment information
- Social security numbers or government IDs
- Passwords or authentication credentials

### Data Storage

Your resume data is stored **locally in your browser** using localStorage. This means:
- Your data never leaves your device
- We cannot access your resume content
- You have full control over your data
- Clearing browser data removes your resume

---

## Security Features

### Browser-Based Security

- **No server-side storage** - Resume data stays in your browser
- **HTTPS everywhere** - All connections are encrypted
- **No third-party tracking** - We don't sell your data
- **Content Security Policy** - Protection against XSS attacks

### AI Import Security

When using AI import features:
- Files are processed and immediately discarded
- No copies are stored on our servers
- AI processing uses encrypted connections

---

## Reporting Vulnerabilities

### Responsible Disclosure

If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it responsibly:

1. **Email:** security@resumefreepro.com (or open a private GitHub issue)
2. **Do not** publicly disclose until we've addressed the issue
3. **Include:** Steps to reproduce, potential impact, suggested fix

### What to Report

- Cross-site scripting (XSS)
- Data exposure vulnerabilities
- Authentication/authorization issues
- Injection vulnerabilities
- Other security concerns

### What Not to Report

- Spam or social engineering attempts
- Denial of service (DoS) attacks
- Missing security headers without proof of exploitability
- Issues in third-party dependencies (report to them directly)

## Best Practices for Users

### Protect Your Data

1. **Use trusted networks** - Avoid public WiFi for sensitive tasks
2. **Keep browser updated** - Security patches matter
3. **Don't share devices** - Your resume is stored locally
4. **Export regularly** - Download your PDF as backup

### Recognize Phishing

ResumeFreePro will **never**:
- Ask for your password via email
- Request payment information
- Send unsolicited attachments
- Ask you to "verify your account"
